Probability Questions

1. A die is rolled once. What is the probability of getting a number greater than 4?

The possible outcomes for rolling a die are {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}. The numbers greater than 4 are {5, 6}, so the probability is 2/6 = 1/3.

2. A bag contains 4 red balls, 5 green balls, and 6 blue balls. What is the probability of drawing a green ball?

The total number of balls is 4 + 5 + 6 = 15. The probability of drawing a green ball is 5/15 = 1/3.

3. Two coins are tossed. What is the probability of getting at least one head?

The possible outcomes are {HH, HT, TH, TT}. Out of these, three outcomes have at least one head, so the probability is 3/4.

4. A box contains 3 red balls, 2 blue balls, and 5 green balls. If one ball is picked at random, what is the probability of getting a red ball?

The total number of balls is 3 + 2 + 5 = 10. The probability of getting a red ball is 3/10.

6. A bag contains 8 black balls and 12 white balls. What is the probability of drawing a white ball?

The total number of balls is 8 + 12 = 20. The probability of drawing a white ball is 12/20 = 3/5.

7. A card is drawn from a deck of 52 cards. What is the probability of drawing a red card?

There are 26 red cards in a deck (13 hearts + 13 diamonds). The probability of drawing a red card is 26/52 = 1/2.

8. A box contains 5 red balls, 4 green balls, and 3 yellow balls. What is the probability of drawing a yellow ball?

The total number of balls is 5 + 4 + 3 = 12. The probability of drawing a yellow ball is 3/12 = 1/4.

9. A die is rolled twice. What is the probability of getting a sum of 7?

The possible outcomes for a sum of 7 are: (1, 6), (2, 5), (3, 4), (4, 3), (5, 2), and (6, 1). There are 6 favorable outcomes out of 36, so the probability is 6/36 = 1/6.

10. What is the probability of drawing a card that is either a spade or a king from a deck of 52 cards?

There are 13 spades and 4 kings in a deck. However, the king of spades is counted twice, so the total number of favorable outcomes is 13 + 4 - 1 = 16. The probability is 16/52 = 4/13.

11. A box contains 10 balls numbered from 1 to 10. What is the probability of drawing an even number?

The even numbers are {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}, so there are 5 favorable outcomes. The probability is 5/10 = 1/2.

12. A jar contains 7 red marbles, 6 blue marbles, and 3 green marbles. What is the probability of drawing a blue marble?

The total number of marbles is 7 + 6 + 3 = 16. The probability of drawing a blue marble is 6/16 = 3/8.

13. What is the probability of drawing a card that is a queen from a standard deck of cards?

14. A bag contains 5 red marbles and 7 blue marbles. If one marble is picked, what is the probability of picking a blue marble?

The total number of marbles is 5 + 7 = 12. The probability of picking a blue marble is 7/12.

15. A coin is tossed three times. What is the probability of getting exactly two heads?

The possible outcomes are {HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT}. There are 3 outcomes with exactly two heads, so the probability is 3/8.

16. A deck of 52 cards is shuffled. What is the probability of drawing an Ace?

There are 4 aces in a deck of 52 cards. The probability is 4/52 = 1/13.

17. A die is rolled twice. What is the probability of getting a sum of 5?

The possible outcomes for a sum of 5 are: (1, 4), (2, 3), (3, 2), and (4, 1). There are 4 favorable outcomes out of 36, so the probability is 4/36 = 1/9.

There are 4 queens and 4 kings in a deck of 52 cards. The probability is (4 + 4) / 52 = 8/52 = 2/13.

19. A box contains 10 balls numbered 1 to 10. What is the probability of drawing a number divisible by 3?

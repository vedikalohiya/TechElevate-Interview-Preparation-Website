Profit and Loss Questions

1. A shopkeeper bought an item for $400 and sold it for $500. What is the profit percentage?

The profit percentage is 25%. (Profit = 500 - 400 = 100; Profit percentage = 100/400 * 100 = 25%).

2. A person sold an item for $2000, making a loss of 20%. What was the cost price?

The cost price was $2500. (Selling Price = Cost Price - 20% of Cost Price; 2000 = Cost Price * (1 - 0.2); Cost Price = 2000/0.8 = 2500).
